My son has a late 90s Jeep Cherokee (XJ) with a pretty rare leather interior option. Problem was, after 25+ years, his front seats were looking pretty tragic. So new leather upholstery for the Jeep was a Christmas present and on an unseasonably warm January day we put it in.

As I looked at all the now-discarded leather, it occurred to me that surely I could salvage some of it and make something for him. So I did.

  {% figure img_path "salvaging.jpeg", "Step one was cutting any usable leather from the old seats. This
   took awhile." %}
  {% figure img_path "airtag.jpeg", "And eventually I had a wrist strap and airtag holder keychain made from the old leather, backed with some new green leather to match the exterior of the Jeep." %}

  My stitching was terrible on this early project, but it was still appreciated.
