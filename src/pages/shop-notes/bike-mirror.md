Over the years, I've bought at least four of these [Origin 8 handlebars](https://amzn.to/4rH1uju). They're attractive, comfortable, versatile, and cheap.

But they have an unusually small internal diameter, which makes finding mirrors that fit very difficult. Most all are made for bars with a larger inner diameter. 

And also, most bike mirrors are as ugly as homemade sin, or so I'm told.

So, armed with my new Bambu printer, I set about creating one that solved both of those problems.

  {% figure img_path "mirror3.jpeg", "The mirror side." %}

  {% figure img_path "mirror2.jpeg", "The front side. The logo was just for fun." %}

[The model is on MakerWorld now](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2454843-bike-mirror-for-small-internal-diameter-bars#profileId-2695350).

For the print, I used this nice [bone ivory PETG](https://amzn.to/4sadg5x). PETG is a must for something like this, that needs to be strong and UV-resistant. I used [this mirror](https://amzn.to/3OAlvJT), although most any 3" mirror should more or less work.
